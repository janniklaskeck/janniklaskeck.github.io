Schacht

Schacht is a competitive 3D platformer racing game. Run on walls, swing, slide and dash to beat the levels as fast as possible. Compare your highscores with players from all around the world or challenge your friends in the local splitscreen multiplayer!
Schacht originally was created over the course of 3 semesters by students in a game project course. Over 30 students participated in creating this game almost wholly on their own.
After release on Steam, the game was further developed, polished and ported to the Playstation 4 by a few core members of the original team.
The Game was developed with the Unreal Engine 4 in C++ and Blueprints.

Steam PS Store

My Project Roles

I participated in the engineering department of this project twice, in the first and third semester.

First Semester:
I worked on several prototypes of the player's abilities, including the first version of the Grappling and Dash.
Other tasks included UI and several miscellaneous issues.

Since this was my first Project with the Unreal Engine 4 i started out with scripting in Blueprints but transitioned later to using mostly C++ where possible to accomplish my tasks.


Third Semester:
I joined in as the one of three programmers to the team of a total of 10 people.
Much of the game was refined and polished in the second semester and the main gameplay was established.
My main tasks this semester were:
- The character accessory system, which allows the player to customize their character with many accessoires provided by the art department. This includes the menu UI were the player can see and choose all the accessoires.
- Creation of a proper Input Settings system, with rebinding of keyboard and (limited)gamepad buttons and several presets to choose from. This also included the menu UI in the settings.
- A Procedural Database of all the segments for the procedural level creation and also the level editor. The database consists of all the segments wrapped in meta data files, contaning a UID and several meta data.

Additionally I worked on lots of smaller programming tasks regarding almost all areas of the game.

I used almost exclusively C++ to accomplish my tasks, except where it was impractible(i.e. creating the UI).
